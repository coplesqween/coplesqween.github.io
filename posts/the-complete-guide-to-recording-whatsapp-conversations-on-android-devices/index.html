2. Adjust settings to enable recording#

In order to record WhatsApp conversations on an Android device, the user needs to make some adjustments to phone settings. To start, access Settings on the device to enter the main configuration menu. Scroll down to the bottom and select Apps to access the App Management window. Select WhatsApp from the list of applications and tap on Permissions. Enable the toggle switch that says Storage to give the app permission to access external storage. With that complete, the user is now free to start recording WhatsApp conversations on their Android device.

Next, turn on the recording feature on the device. Access the Recording Settings window and toggle the switch next to WhatsApp. This allows the recording feature to run in the background while the user is conversing. Once recording permission has been granted, the user can start recording immediately by pressing the Record button when prompted.

To keep the recordings private, the user should also adjust the privacy settings. Access the Privacy Settings window and toggle the Allow Recording switch. This ensures that only the user can access the recordings. To finish, select the Save button to save the new settings.

With the recording feature enabled, the user is now ready to start recording WhatsApp conversations on their Android device. All conversations will be accessible from the Recording Manager window. From here, users can review their recordings and save them to a secure location.

1. Enabling Recording on WhatsApp#

Before you can start recording your WhatsApp conversations, you’ll first need to adjust your device settings. First, go into your device settings menu, then tap on ‘Security & location’. Look for the ‘Device Admin Apps’ option, and select ‘WhatsApp Recorder’. This will enable the app for recording. Once enabled, you’ll be able to record all incoming and outgoing WhatsApp conversations.

2. Adjusting Settings for Recording#

Before you begin recording WhatsApp conversations on android, you need to adjust settings. Open WhatsApp and tap on settings, then select ‘Data and Storage Usage’. Choose ‘Network Usage’ in the pop-up window, then select ‘Media Auto-Download’ and turn ‘When Using Mobile data’ off. This will ensure that any incoming media, including audios and videos, will not be auto-downloaded.

Next, head back to Data and Storage Usage, and select ‘Storage Usage’ in the pop-up window. Here, you can also delete any unnecessary media to free up space on your device.

Once these steps are completed, open the conversation you want to record. Then, you can add a call to the conversation and select the ‘voice call’ option. Make sure the other person joins the call before you tap the ‘Add Participant’ button. Now that the other person has joined the call, tap the ‘record’ button and the voice call will be recorded.

Finally, tap the ‘Record’ button again to stop recording the conversation and have it saved on your device. You can access your recordings from the WhatsApp Audio folder on your device’s storage.

These steps will help you enable recording for WhatsApp conversations on your Android device.
