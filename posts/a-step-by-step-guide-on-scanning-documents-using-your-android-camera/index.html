2. How to Scan Documents using an Android Camera#

Scanners can be bulky and expensive, but did you know your Android phone can also help you scan documents? With a few simple steps, you can quickly scan documents in no time. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you scan documents using your Android camera.

First, open your Google Drive app and select “New”. Next, select the “Scan” option from the drop-down menu. This will open the camera on your Android device. Place the document you wish to scan on a flat surface and point your camera at it. An orange rectangle will appear on the document, showing it has been detected. Use your finger to adjust the corners of the rectangle if necessary.

When you’re ready, tap the checkmark at the bottom of the screen to capture the image. You will have the option to crop the image before saving. Once you’re done editing, select “Save” to save the document to your Google Drive.

Lastly, you can preview the scanned document by tapping the newly created document from your Google Drive. Here, you can further edit the document and save it in different file formats. With these steps, you can easily scan documents using your Android camera.

3. Tips and Troubleshooting#

Scanning documents can be done from the comfort of your own home with an Android phone. The process is simple and straightforward, and here are some tips and troubleshooting to make the process easier.

First, make sure you have a device with a good enough camera to produce decent results. Check the specifications of your device for the megapixel rating of the camera.

Second, make sure you have good lighting to get the best possible scan. Move closer to a window or use an external light source to give your images the best quality.

Third, steady your hand and hold your phone as parallel to the document as possible. You want the scan to be as accurate as possible and this will help to achieve a crisper image.

Finally, take multiple scans of the document. This will allow you to have multiple images to work with and discard the ones that don’t look as good.

2. Scanning Documents#

When it comes to scanning documents on an Android device, the process couldn’t be simpler. Using the camera on your device, you can quickly and easily capture a digital version of your physical documents, without having to invest in a dedicated scanner. Here’s how to scan documents on an Android device.

First, open the Camera app on your device and focus the device’s camera on the document you want to scan. The camera should automatically adjust the exposure and contrast, so you don’t need to worry about adjusting those settings yourself. When you’re satisfied with the photo, press the shutter button to save the image.

Next, go to the Google Drive app on your Android device and open the document you just scanned. Tap the ‘Edit’ button on the upper right of the screen and select ‘Scan document’ from the resulting menu. The app will automatically detect the edges and adjust the borders to define the document.

Finally, tap the ‘Save’ button at the bottom right of the screen. The scanned document will be saved to your device’s camera roll and you can access it from Google Drive anytime.

These simple steps will help you scan documents quickly and easily on your Android device. With the help of the camera and Google Drive apps, you don’t need to purchase a separate scanner to capture digital versions of your physical documents.
