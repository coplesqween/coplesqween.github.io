Find Advanced and tap on it. Lastly, tap on Developer Options to activate it. After it is enabled, you should find a new menu in the System option.

From there, you need to enable two options - USB debugging and Take a bug report. Both need to be ticked in order to take screenshots without the use of apps. Once you’re done, you can now take screenshots without an app and have access to additional developer options.

To take a screenshot, you need to press the Power and Volume Down buttons simultaneously. The screenshot will be saved in the screenshots folder of your Gallery App. For some devices, you may need to press the Power and Home buttons instead.

2. Select USB Debugging#

Take screenshot on your smartphone can be tricky if the application doesn’t allow you to do it. Fortunately, there is tiny workaround that you can do to make it work. First, you must enable USB debugging in your Android phone. You can find it in the Developer options in Settings. Next, connect your phone with a USB cable to your computer and launch the Android SDK Platform-tools on your computer. After that, enter the following command in the command prompt: “adb shell screencap -p /sdcard/screenshot.png”. Finally, type “adb pull /sdcard/screenshot.png” and your screenshot will be stored in the same directory as the Android SDK Platform tools.
